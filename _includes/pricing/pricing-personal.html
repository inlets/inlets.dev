Support for inlets

We'll help you get it working

If you'd like to evaluate inlets for business purposes, you will need to start on the Starter Plan for Professionals, which is available on a monthly basis. If you're an individual user you may be able to purchase a personal license (but check the terms and conditions below).

When purchased annually, the business licenses include a free onboarding call and priority support via email. Personal licenses are self-service only, however you can raise a GitHub issues if you think something is not working as it should.

Plans for individual developers

Do I need a business or personal license?

A business license is required for evaluation for business purposes, can be expensed, ordered with a work email address and shared with other members of the team.

A personal license can be used by an employee for use at work and at home, however you must pay with your own money and a personal email address.

Can I use a personal license to evaluate inlets for my business?
You will require a business license, however it is available on a monthly basis for evaluation, see Starter Plan for Professionals.
Can I use a personal license to set up a permanent tunnel or integration?
You may only do this at home, for personal usage, not at work or for your employer. You can set up temporary tunnels at work for testing and development of company products.
How do I expense a personal license?
You must pay for a personal license with your own money, and this cannot be expensed. Business licenses can be expensed.
What email address can I use for my personal license?
You'll need to use a personal email address for a personal license. We reserve the right to cancel any personal licenses bought with a commercial email address.
How many people can I share a personal license with?
Only business licenses can be shared between multiple people, as long as no more tunnels are launched than specified in your license.

What kinds of use are acceptable for a personal license?

  • Receiving webhooks during development
  • Hosting personal projects and websites.
  • Remote access to personal servers over SSH, RDP and VNC.
  • Sharing a blog post draft with a colleague or client for feedback.
  • To get a static IP when you're traveling, behind NAT, use Starlink, have a dynamic IP, or when the ports you need are blocked.
  • Self-hosting services from a Kubernetes cluster for development and testing.
  • Accessing dashboards or portal from your Raspberry Pi.
  • To avoid rate-limits and SaaS tunnels, which are often blocked by restrictive networks.

If you have any questions about allowable use for licenses, feel free to get in touch via email.
